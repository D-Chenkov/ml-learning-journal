**Reflection (book report):**

"Building product evals is simply the scientific method in disguise. That's the secret sauce. It's a cycle of inquiry, experimentation, and analysis" was the most important line for me.

The read is surprisingly short and succinct, but it does not have to be convoluted; the core is that the scientific method IS the evals. We are no longer relying on the binary choice of unit tests or integration tests. What we are doing is a careful analysis, hoping that our model does not regress from the ideal state in which we find our current iteration. Of course slight regression is okay, but in the same vein that we sometimes want to break a little to fix a little, the scientific method is the perfect analog to this situation. In the same way I have dealt with products, it is all about iteration.

Eval-driven development is also mentioned here, as a substitute for the good old test-driven development, and it is the perfect fit for AI applications. That is not to say we neglect the core tests and checking that functionality is up, alive, and working. But the metrics we work with are now different. It is not just about lifting up the service; it is about how well the service works, now more than ever.
